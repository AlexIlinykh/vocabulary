bilious

adjective

Show/Hide

of or indicative of a peevish ill-natured disposition; sickeningly unpleasant

"her bilious humor"

Bilious is one of several words whose origins trace to the old belief that four bodily humors (black bile, yellow bile, phlegm, and blood) control temperament. Just like phlegmatic ("of a slow and stolid phlegm-driven character"), melancholy ("experiencing dejection associated with black bile"), and sanguine ("of a cheerful, blood-based disposition"), bilious suggests a personality associated with an excess of one of the humorsâ€”in this case, yellow bile. Such a personality may also be described as unreasonable, peevish, or ill-tempered.
